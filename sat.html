Why Not Private Tutoring?

Cohort Learning Beats 1-on-1

Private tutoring sounds premium, but the research and our results show a small, motivated cohort produces better outcomes. Here’s why.

01

Peer Accountability

Students who study alongside peers of similar ambition show up prepared, complete homework consistently, and push each other to improve. 1-on-1 sessions can’t replicate that energy.

02

Collaborative Problem-Solving

Seeing how other students approach the same question, and where they get stuck, builds deeper understanding than watching a tutor solve it alone. You learn the patterns, not just the answers.

03

Real Test Conditions

The SAT is taken in a room full of students under pressure. Our cohort format builds comfort performing alongside peers, so test day feels familiar.

04

Built for Results, Not Markup

Private tutoring charges a premium for a format that’s actually less effective. Cohort pricing reflects the model: you’re paying for outcomes, not overhead.

Common Questions

What Parents Ask

How big are the cohorts?

Cohorts are intentionally small, typically 6–10 students. That gives every student meaningful attention from the instructor and enough peers to create genuine collaborative energy.

What if my student misses a session?

Every live session is recorded and paired with async modules covering the same material. Students who miss a session can catch up between classes without falling behind.

Is this the digital SAT or paper SAT?

Our curriculum is built entirely around the digital SAT, the format used for all SAT administrations starting in 2024. Students practice in the exact conditions they’ll encounter on test day.

Is the course enough on its own, or do we need supplemental tutoring?

For most students, the 8-week course plus async practice is sufficient to reach their target score. Students with specific weak areas can add supplemental 1-on-1 sessions on request, but the majority don’t need it.

What’s the expected time commitment between sessions?

Plan for roughly 3–5 hours of self-directed practice per week: async modules, timed sections, and at least one full-length practice test every other week. Students who put in the work see the biggest gains.

Who are the instructors?

Every Montara SAT instructor scored in the 99th percentile on the SAT and was vetted through a rigorous hiring process. Most are graduate students at top universities who understand both the test and how to teach it.
